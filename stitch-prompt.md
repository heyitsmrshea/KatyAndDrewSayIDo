Create a single-page wedding website for "Katy & Drew Say I Do" focused primarily on the itinerary for a Cancun wedding week. This is not a general wedding homepage. It is an itinerary-first guest guide with RSVP functionality built into the page.

The trip runs from Tuesday through Sunday, and Sunday is the wedding day.

Primary goals:
- Make the itinerary the clear centerpiece of the site.
- Let guests quickly understand what is happening each day.
- Let guests RSVP by entering their name and selecting which events they will attend.
- Keep the page beautiful, premium, and easy to scan.

Overall design direction:
- Whimsical, romantic, sun-soaked, and editorial.
- Luxury destination wedding energy, not a default wedding template.
- Avoid cliché rustic wedding styling, generic blush florals, scrapbook design, or overly soft template aesthetics.
- The site should feel elevated and intentional, with strong hierarchy and polished spacing.
- Think whimsical beach-resort elegance mixed with modern editorial layout.

Color and visual mood:
- Light blue, lavender, and peach should drive the visual palette.
- Use very soft supporting neutrals like airy ivory, petal pink, or pale lilac-tinted whites if needed.
- Bright and airy, not dark.
- Use atmospheric backgrounds, gradients, subtle texture, and shaped visual layers rather than flat white sections.

Typography:
- High-end serif for major headlines.
- Clean modern sans-serif for itinerary details, labels, and RSVP UI.
- Prioritize readability for schedule information.

The page should include these sections:

1. Hero section
- Title: "Katy & Drew Say I Do"
- Supporting line that clearly says this is the Cancun wedding itinerary and guest guide.
- Include Cancun, Mexico.
- Include a CTA that jumps directly to the itinerary section.
- The hero should feel premium and romantic, but still clearly orient the guest around the week ahead.

2. Week overview
- A concise summary that explains the celebration runs Tuesday through Sunday.
- Make it explicit that Sunday is the wedding day.
- Include an at-a-glance overview of the week so guests can immediately understand the flow.

3. Main itinerary section
- This is the most important section on the page.
- The layout should be highly readable and visually polished.
- Create structured itinerary blocks for:
  - Tuesday: arrivals and resort check-in
  - Wednesday: welcome event / group activity / relaxed resort day
  - Thursday: excursion / optional plans / group time
  - Friday: wedding weekend energy builds / guest plans
  - Saturday: rehearsal / welcome party / pre-wedding gathering
  - Sunday: wedding day
- Each day should include:
  - day label
  - event title
  - time
  - location
  - short description
  - attire or notes
- Some days can have multiple events.
- The design should feel like a refined itinerary system, not a stack of plain cards.

4. Sunday wedding day spotlight
- Sunday must stand out visually from the other days.
- Give Sunday a more elevated, emotional, and romantic presentation.
- Include ceremony, cocktails, dinner, dancing, and celebration flow.
- This section should feel like the page’s visual and emotional peak.

5. RSVP section
- Add a dedicated RSVP area on the same page.
- Guests should be able to enter their name.
- Guests should be able to select which events they are attending across the week.
- This should support RSVP choices for multiple events, not just the wedding itself.
- Example event choices can include:
  - Wednesday welcome event
  - Thursday group outing
  - Saturday welcome party
  - Sunday wedding ceremony and reception
- The RSVP design should feel integrated with the site, not like a generic web form dropped onto the page.
- It should feel clean, polished, and easy to complete.
- Include a submit button and a short confirmation message area or success state.

6. Travel and stay details
- Include concise guest logistics such as airport, transportation, hotel or resort stay, and packing reminders.
- Present this in a scannable and polished layout.

7. Helpful details / FAQ
- Include practical guest questions around arrival timing, transportation, dress code, optional events, and what to bring.
- Keep it easy to skim.

8. Closing section
- End with a warm note from Katy and Drew.
- Make the closing feel personal and celebratory.

Layout guidance:
- The page should feel dynamic and art directed.
- Alternate compositions so the site does not become repetitive.
- Use editorial panels, elevated itinerary modules, soft layered backgrounds, and premium spacing.
- Avoid making every section look like identical rectangular boxes.
- The page must look strong on both desktop and mobile.

Functionality guidance:
- The itinerary is the main feature.
- RSVP is the second main feature.
- RSVP should clearly connect to the itinerary, so guests feel like they are responding to specific events during the week.
- Build the RSVP interaction in a way that could reasonably support form submission.

Content guidance:
- Use tasteful placeholder copy where needed.
- Keep the tone warm, polished, inviting, and destination-specific.
- Do not make the site feel corporate or like a conference event page.

Important:
- This is a single-page site.
- The design must be itinerary-forward.
- Sunday is the wedding day and should receive the strongest visual emphasis.
- The RSVP must allow guests to enter their name and choose attendance for different events during the week.
- The final result should feel like a world-class destination wedding itinerary microsite.
